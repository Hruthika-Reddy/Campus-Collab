23HS1102 - Indian Constitution

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive understanding of the Indian Constitution, its institutions, and governance processes. It covers constitutional principles, government structures, and the judicial system to foster awareness about India's democratic framework.

Course Details

Course Category L-T-P Credits
MC 2-0-0 0

Course Learning Objectives

  1. Understand constitutional institutions and their functioning
  2. Analyze societal governance processes systematically
  3. Appreciate principles of Social Justice, Liberty, Equity, and Fraternity
  4. Introduce the concept of political systems
  5. Examine working processes of constitutional institutions
  6. Create awareness about India's judicial system

Course Contents

UNIT I: Introduction (5 Contact Hours)

  • Meaning of Constitution
  • Indian Constitution: Sources and history
  • Key Features: Citizenship, Preamble
  • Fundamental Rights and Duties
  • Directive Principles of State Policy

UNIT II: Union Government (5 Contact Hours)

  • Federal structure and Centre-State relations
  • President: Role, powers, and position
  • PM and Council of Ministers
  • Cabinet and Central Secretariat
  • Parliament: Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha

UNIT III: Election Commission (5 Contact Hours)

  • Election Commission: Role and functioning
  • Chief Election Commissioner
  • State Election Commission
  • Welfare institutions for SC/ST/OBC and women

UNIT IV: State Government (3 Contact Hours)

  • Governor: Role and position
  • CM and Council of Ministers
  • State Secretariat: Organization and functions

UNIT V: Local Administration (7 Contact Hours)

  • District Administration and its head
  • Municipalities: Mayor and elected representatives
  • Panchayati Raj System
  • Zilla Panchayat and Block level hierarchy
  • Village level administration
  • Grassroot democracy importance

UNIT VI: Union Judiciary (5 Contact Hours)

  • Supreme Court: Establishment and constitution
  • Appointment of Judges
  • High Courts: Establishment and jurisdiction
  • WRITS and Public Interest Litigation (PIL)
